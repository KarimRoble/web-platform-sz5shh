    • Camilids

    The four ilamas and two camels are camilids Camlids tend to be larger and are strictly herbivorous. Camilids differ from ruminants in several ways. They have a three-chambered rather than a four-chambered digestive tract; an upper lip that is split in two with each part separatly mobile; and an isolated incisor in the upper jaw. they have long legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still. (from Wikipedia)
